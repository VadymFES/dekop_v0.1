# Data Protection Officer (DPO) Appointment

## Overview

As per Draft Law No. 8153 "On Protection of Personal Data" (adopted November 20, 2024), **all businesses processing personal data in Ukraine will be required to appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO)** once the law is adopted in 2025.

This document outlines the DPO role, responsibilities, and appointment process for Dekop Furniture Enterprise.

---

## Legal Requirement

**Law**: Draft Law No. 8153 (expected adoption: Q2 2025)
**Applies to**: All organizations processing personal data
**Deadline**: Upon law adoption (prepare now)
**Penalty**: Fines up to 5% of annual turnover (minimum UAH 300,000)

---

## DPO Responsibilities

### Primary Duties

1. **Compliance Monitoring**
   - Monitor adherence to data protection laws
   - Regular compliance audits
   - Track data processing activities
   - Review and update policies

2. **Advisory Function**
   - Advise management on data protection obligations
   - Provide guidance on DPIAs (Data Protection Impact Assessments)
   - Support privacy by design initiatives
   - Advise on data security measures

3. **Liaison with Authorities**
   - Act as primary contact for Ombudsman
   - Cooperate with supervisory authority
   - Facilitate inspections and audits
   - Report data breaches (within 72 hours)

4. **Employee Training**
   - Conduct data protection training
   - Raise awareness of GDPR/Ukrainian law requirements
   - Maintain training records
   - Regular refresher courses

5. **Data Subject Rights**
   - Handle data subject requests (access, deletion, rectification)
   - Ensure 30-day response timeline compliance
   - Maintain request logs
   - Resolve complaints

## DPO Appointment Options

### Option 1: Internal DPO (Recommended for Dekop)

**Pros:**
- Better understanding of business operations
- Immediate availability
- Lower cost
- Direct access to systems and data

**Cons:**
- Requires training and certification
- Time commitment from existing staff
- Potential conflict of interest (must be independent)

**Suitable for**: Small to medium businesses (like Dekop)

### Option 2: External DPO Consultant

**Pros:**
- Expert knowledge from day one
- No conflict of interest
- Flexible engagement
- Handles multiple clients

**Cons:**
- Higher cost (monthly retainer)
- Less familiar with business specifics
- Remote availability only

**Suitable for**: Organizations lacking internal expertise

### Option 3: Shared DPO Service

**Pros:**
- Cost-effective for small businesses
- Professional expertise
- Compliance guaranteed

**Cons:**
- Shared attention
- May not be as responsive
- Less customized approach

**Suitable for**: Very small businesses with limited budget

---

## Recommended Approach for Dekop

**Stage 1 (Now - Q1 2025): Preparation**
- Designate interim DPO (e.g., Technical Lead or CTO)
- Begin DPO training/certification
- Document current data processing activities
- Implement compliance procedures

**Stage 2 (Q2 2025 - Law Adoption): Official Appointment**
- Formally appoint DPO
- Publish DPO contact information
- Notify authorities (if required)
- Implement DPO reporting structure

**Stage 3 (Post-Adoption): Ongoing Operations**
- Regular compliance monitoring
- Quarterly management reports
- Annual compliance audit
- Continuous training and improvement

## DPO Training Resources

### Online Courses
1. **IAPP CIPP/E Certification** (Europe)
   - https://iapp.org/certify/cipp/europe/
   - Gold standard for data protection professionals
   - Cost: ~$500-700

2. **GDPR Practitioner Course** (Ukrainian)
   - Available through Ukrainian law firms
   - 40-80 hours training
   - Specific to Ukrainian context

3. **Online GDPR Courses** (Free)
   - EU GDPR training modules
   - Coursera GDPR courses
   - LinkedIn Learning data privacy courses

### Ukrainian Resources
- Уповноважений з прав людини website: https://ombudsman.gov.ua
- Ukrainian GDPR Association training
- Legal webinars on Draft Law 8153
